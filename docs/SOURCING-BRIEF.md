## Channel pick (per product type)
- **Stainless commodity** (cross, mustard seed, spinner, dove): Alibaba bulk for cost, or
  **US-warehouse dropship** to hit fast shipping + no-tarnish:
  - **Alamode** (Garden Grove CA, since 1980): TK316 surgical steel + 925 silver,
    **$7 flat dropship fee incl. jewelry box + packaging**.
  - **CJ Dropshipping** US warehouse: realistic **~7–17 day** delivery (beats 21–28 overseas).
- **Engravable verse / quiz-personalized**: POD engraving — but **confirm fulfillment
  time + cost directly** (ShineOn's "2-5 day / 65% margin" claim was REFUTED).
- **Saint medals**: CJ or Alibaba, **order samples + verify 316L grade** first.

## Two-audience note
- **US general Christian**: cross, scripture/verse, mustard seed, dove/ichthys, spinner ring.
- **US Latin/Hispanic Catholic** (distinct, documented base): San Judas/St Jude, Virgen de
  Guadalupe, San Benito, Milagrosa, San Miguel. **Bilingual naming** ("San Judas," "Medalla
  de San Juditas") and **provenance/accuracy** are trust differentiators in this niche.
- Gifting windows (confirmed): **baptism, First Communion, quinceañera**, birthdays,
  Mother's/Father's Day, Christmas, Valentine's.

## ⚠️ Risk flags
- **Do NOT market "waterproof / ~10-year PVD" durability** — those claims were REFUTED
  (0-3). PVD gold still wears; over-claiming = refund risk that kills the trust brand.
- **Trademark**: avoid the exact registered phrases **"Holy Family Hearts"** and **"Holy
  Heart"** on jewelry (The Little Catholic, USPTO). Generic devotional terms (Sacred Heart,
  Immaculate Heart) were rejected as generic → usually safe.
- **QC**: cheap steel mislabeled as 316L is documented — **sample + verify grade before scaling.**
- **Religious accuracy**: correct Guadalupe/saint iconography matters to devout Latin buyers.

## Must-verify before scaling (open questions)
1. Real PVD-gold durability on the specific SKUs (microns / salt-spray) — sample test.
2. Real non-sale per-SKU cost, MOQ, lead time for saint medals from CJ US warehouse vs Alibaba.
3. True POD engraving fulfillment time + per-unit cost (ShineOn claim refuted).
4. Approved Guadalupe/saint imagery / any licensing expectations for a devout audience.
